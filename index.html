Little trivia about me: I sung with famous Irish musician Glen Hansard on stage a few times.

When I’m not behind a screen or playing music, you can find me outdoors skateboarding or trekking.

Water Squirrel

Game App / UX Design

Water Squirrel is an immersive single-player indie game. The goal is to help the Water Squirrel find a giant acorn by avoiding obstacles along the way.

Find out more about this project on Behance: Water Squirrel Behance Project

IDEATION

For this project, I explored the quirky idea of a squirrel underwater.
Parallax Scrolling, a technique used in computer graphics where foreground images move by quicker than the ones in the background, also inspired this project giving a sense of immersion and an illusion of depth.

DESIGN

A user-centered approach was taken with the creation of a persona, scenarios and use cases.

All vector game graphics and icons were designed by me from scratch for the game using Adobe Illustrator and Sketch.

GAME DEVELOPMENT

Water Squirrel app was developed in Lua using Corona SDK, which provides a cross-platform mobile development platform especially suited for 2D games.

Additionally, I created an animated sprite for the launch screen and added a theme tune and audio effects to the game to make it more appealing.

VML Technology

Product / UX / CX

After working as a full-stack product engineer at VML Technology for 5 years, I transitioned to a Customer & User Experience Executive role.
I collaborate with all teams to ensure that UX is well integrated across all phases of product development.
My role involves developing new product concepts, competitive analysis, conducting user research and design research while collaborating with all teams to ensure that UX is well-integrated into the product development cycle.

Additionally, I lead weekly Design Reviews with the design and tech teams in order to feedback on the technical feasibility of designs and ensure that the teams’ objectives are aligned with the user’s and company’s goals.

Working closely with customers to help them make the most of the patented video technology also allows me to also understand our target audience better, refine our user personas and identify more use cases for the technology.
Integrating external data into the core product and providing technical support to clients is my responsibility.

I’m currently working on the upcoming advanced video editor and I’ve been putting together knowledge base content and onboarding material for customers.

PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT CYCLE

When a new feature is requested, as the initial part of the design process, I liaise with the CTO and Head of Design on requirements. Then, I create the information architecture mapping and visual low-fi mock-ups to validate and discuss the ideas to the design team. Next step is to plan out the tasks in detail for the design team alongside the Head of Design. Then designs will go through various iterations based on initial internal reviews.

Once a working prototype is ready, we do some testing to validate our assumptions and learn more about the user’s reactions when interacting with it. This research is then evaluated and will feed into the design decisions. Iterations might occur again based on the outcome of testing before designs are signed off for development.

When designs are handed over to the development team, I make sure all that all the design decisions and requirements are followed through. As product updates are released, I test them out to ensure the UX is as expected and raise any bugs I may come across during testing.

Visualise It

Web Application / Data Visualisation

Visualise It is an experimental user-friendly web data visualisation tool which automatically analyses a dataset and generates meaningful data visualisations for it.

BACKGROUND RESEARCH

I developed this application as my final research project for my Bachelor’s Degree in Information Science for Management.

At the time of research, existing systems were mainly desktop applications which focused only on relational data sets and required prior knowledge or training.
Visualise It was born from the need for an easily accessible data visualisation tool that anyone could use to analyse datasets.

DEVELOPMENT

I developed Visualise It using web technologies such as HTML5, CSS3, SVG, D3.js, Google Maps API and Bootstrap.
With Visualise It, users can upload data in one of three formats (XML, csv or JSON). Then, data is analysed, filtered and presented visually to the user.

Then, data is analysed, filtered and presented visually. The application automatically generates different visualisations best suitable for the submitted data. The data analysis is done through an API called “The Semantic Detector”, developed by fellow Computer Science graduate Gabriele Cigna.

Based on the API results, Visualise It then filters the data and presents it visually. The various visualisations generated are only the ones best suitable for the submitted data.
User interactions with data, such as choosing a graph’s variables and animations are a crucial aspect of the application giving a tool to the users to seamlessly speculate and analyse their data.

Given the broad scope and experimental nature of this project, Visualise It isn’t a definitive solution to the Information Visualisation problem but an attempt to solve it in an innovative way.

Picseed

iOS App / UX / User Research

Picseed is an iOS App which allows to capture, collect and share inspiration.
Content is tailored to the user’s interests to give a personalised experience, inspire and motivate them.
Following the agile methodology, user experience has been at the forefront of this project, driving each UX refinement iteration.

Find out more about this project on Behance: Picseed Behance Project

TEAM WORK & CONCEPT

Picseed was born from a mutual passion for art and collaboration with Lisa Reburn, talented colleague and friend.

The application was created as the final project of our MSc in Creative Digital Media in 2016 and was awarded top marks.

Our plan was to create an app to aid creatives in their inspiration process. User research and market analysis revealed there was a need for a such a tool.

Shown below are the affinity diagram we put together from our initial user research and some initial sketches I did.

USER TESTING

User testing was crucial to Picseed’s development and refinement.
We carried out frequent user testing sessions both in-person and remotely as well as card sorting, A&B tests and focus groups.

The feedback gathered allowed us to refine the app and track issues in order to promptly find the best solution using the latest design standards and state-of-art technologies.

DEVELOPING PICSEED

Developing Picseed was at times challenging but overall a rewarding experience.
Data modeling and code design at various stages of development was fundamental in achieving the application’s requirements.

Content is sourced from reputable art content providers such as Behance and hand-picked curation art Tumblr blogs.

PICSEED WEBSITE

Before launching Picseed’s beta testing, we decided to create a web page for the app to promote the app and give Picseed an online presence.

Lisa took care of the web design and I coded the website using Bootstrap and the latest web technologies.
