Hello, I'm Silvia.

I’m an Italian hybrid designer / developer, photographer & singer-songwriter.
Sylvy is my stage name.

After graduating in Information Technology at the University of Bologna, I moved to Ireland to study at the Dublin Institute of Technology where I achieved a First-class Honours MsC in Creative Digital Media specialising in Mobile & Web Applications.

Currently based in Northern Ireland, I work as a Customer & User Experience Lead at VML Technology , where I come up with new UX design product ideas, lead user research and carry out design research, collaborating with all teams ensuring that UX is well-integrated into the product development flow.

I’m passionate about creating and improving digital products through research and testing to suit the user’s needs and the objectives of the business. I have an eye for detail and I’m always eager to embrace new challenges with an open mind and a positive attitude.

I mentored at the successful first edition of LearnTo iOS, an eight-week iOS development course for students and professionals.
I've also volunteered at Django Girls Belfast to support and inspire other women in technology.

I’ve been practising photography for 10 years working on different genres. My favourite ones are music, landscape and abstract photography. I constantly experiment in my creative process with both digital and analog mediums.

I released “The Starry-Eyed”, a small collection of songs written by me, under my stage name Sylvy. My music is available to listen on all online platforms.
Little trivia about me: I sung with famous Irish musician Glen Hansard on stage a few times.

When I’m not behind a screen or playing music, you can find me outdoors skateboarding or trekking.

Remix

Identity / Branding / Website

Brand identity and website for a fictitious newborn independent record label named Remix.

BRAND IDENTITY

The design process involved initial brainstorming, market research and the creation of an inspirational mood board. I did a brain dump on paper for the identity and developed the most appealing ideas on Illustrator until Remix logo came to life.

WEB DESIGN

For Remix website, I sketched ideas, planned its content and a site map. From the identity, colour palette and typography were created and they were used in the wireframes.

I designed an additional ‘Store’ page along with merchandise including clothing and guitar picks.

Since the record label was at its initial stage of finding talents, a ‘Connect’ page was created with this in mind as it allows artists to send their demos directly to Remix.

A mobile version of the website was also designed and the logo was adapted as an iOS app logo.

WEB DEVELOPMENT

Remix website was coded using HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, jQuery, Javascript and PHP (server-side).

Project: Remix

Water Squirrel

Game App

Water Squirrel is an immersive single-player game where the player takes control over the actions of the character. The aim is to help the water squirrel find a giant acorn by avoiding obstacles along the way.

IDEATION

For this project, I explored the quirky idea of a squirrel underwater.
Parallax Scrolling, a technique used in computer graphics where foreground images move by quicker than the ones in the background, also inspired this project giving a sense of immersion and an illusion of depth.

USER-CENTERED DESIGN

A user-centered approach was taken with the creation of a persona, scenarios and use cases.

The App Design process involved the following steps:

  1. Creation of a Flow Diagram that defined the application’s flow as described in the scenarios.
  2. Design of basic UI elements for the game’s interface.

UI DESIGN & GAME DEVELOPMENT

I designed all the graphics from scratch for the game which I then developed in Lua using Corona SDK, which provides a cross-platform mobile development platform especially suited for 2D games.

Additionally, I created an animated sprite for the launch screen and added a theme tune and audio effects to the game to make it more appealing.

Project: Water Squirrel

Resources: iPhone 5s mockup

VML Technology

Product / UX Design

After working as a full-stack product engineer at VML Technology for 5 years, I have transitioned to a Customer & User Experience Lead role. I come up with new UX design product ideas, lead user research and carry out design research, collaborating with all teams ensuring that UX is well-integrated into the product development flow.

Other duties in my job include carrying out competitive analysis and design to stay up to date with the current trends and understand how to adapt to the market. I lead regular Design Reviews with the Design team in order to feedback on the technical feasibility of designs and ensure that our teams’ objectives are aligned with the user’s and company’s goals.

Working with customers to help them make the most of the patented video technology allows me to also understand our target audience better, refine our user personas and identify more use cases for the technology.

I’m currently working on the upcoming advanced video editor as well as putting together supporting documentation and onboarding material for the customers.

PRODUCT DESIGN PROCESS

When a new feature is requested, as the initial part of the design process, I liase with the CTO and Head of Design on requirements. Then, I create the information architecture mapping and visual low-fi mock-ups to validate and discuss the ideas to the design team. Next step is to plan out the tasks in detail for the design team alongside the Head of Design. Then designs will go through various iterations based on initial internal reviews.

Once a working prototype is ready, we do some testing to validate our assumptions and learn more about the user’s reactions when interacting with it. This research is then evaluated and will feed into the design decisions. Iterations might occur again based on the outcome of testing before designs are signed off for development.

When designs are handed over to the development team, I make sure all that all the design decisions and requirements are followed through. As product updates are released, I test them out to ensure the UX is as expected and raise any bugs I may come across during testing.

Visualise It

Web Application / Data Visualisation

Experimental web app that attempts to visualise data in a simple meaningful way and caters for anyone.

BACKGROUND RESEARCH

I developed this web app as my final project for my Bachelor’s Degree in Information Science for Management.
The goal was to create a tool for automatic generation of Open Data visualisations.

At the time of research, existing systems were mainly desktop applications which focused only on relational data sets and required prior knowledge or training. Visualise It was born from the need for a universal and easily accessible data visualisation tool.

DEVELOPMENT

I developed Visualise It with web technologies such as HTML5, CSS3, SVG, D3.js, Google Maps API and Bootstrap.
The semantic data sets analysis is done by The Semantic Detector an API created by Computer Science graduate Gabriele Cigna.

With Visualise It, users can upload data in one of three formats (XML, csv or JSON).
Then, data is analysed, filtered and presented visually. The application automatically generates different visualisations best suitable for the submitted data.

Interactions with data, such as choosing a graph’s variables and animations are a crucial aspect of the application giving a tool to the users to seamlessly speculate and analyse their data.

Given the broad scope and experimental nature of this project, Visualise It isn’t a definitive solution to the Information Visualisation problem but an attempt to solve it in an innovative way.

Project: Visualise It

Resources: Macbook mockup, Safari Browser mockup

Picseed

iOS App / UX Design & Research

Picseed is an iOS App which allows to capture, collect and share inspiration.
Content is tailored to the user’s interests to give a personalised experience, inspire and motivate them.

TEAM WORK & CONCEPT

The app was created for my Masters project. Picseed was born from a mutual passion for art and collaboration with Lisa Reburn, talented colleague and friend.

Our plan was to create an app to aid creatives in their inspiration process. User research and market analysis revealed there was a need for a such a tool.

Shown below are the affinity diagram we put together from our initial user research and some initial sketches I did.

USER TESTING

User testing was crucial to Picseed’s development and refinement.
We carried out frequent user testing sessions including remote testing, card sorting and focus groups.

The feedback gathered allowed us to refine the app and track issues in order to promptly find the best solution using the latest design standards and state-of-art technologies.

DEVELOPING PICSEED

Developing Picseed was at times challenging but overall a rewarding experience.

PICSEED WEBSITE

Before launching Picseed’s beta testing, we decided to create a web page for the app to promote the app and give Picseed an online presence.

Lisa took care of the web design and I coded the website using Bootstrap and the latest web technologies.
